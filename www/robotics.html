  • ENGRAVER

    Can your robot be artistic ? ENGRAVER is an event in which teams will have to make a robot which will be used to draw. Come and test your technical, controlling and innovation skills along with a lot of fun!
    PROBLEM STATEMENT :
    Event consists of a robot with a proper mechanism to draw/write (marker paint etc.). In ENGRAVER, you will have to write the word ‘MICROBIRD’ by controlling your robot, in minimum time to beat others. This means that the path to be traced will be alphabets. While moving from one alphabet to the next, the drawing mechanism should be off and should again begin at the start of the next alphabet.
    GENERAL RULES:
    1. Each team can have a maximum of 4 participants.
    2. A team may consist of students from different colleges.
    3. A maximum of 2 chances will be given to each team.
    4. No damage should be made by a bot to other teams' bots during the whole event, in any manner.
    5. Bots should not be disassembled until the results are declared.
    6. The organizers reserve the right to change the rules as they deem fit.
    7. When a team is called for the task, they must report within five minutes.
    8. The decision of judges will be final.

    ROBOT CONTROL AND SPECIFICATIONS:
    1. The Robot must be stable and able to move on its own.
    2. During the whole event, the robot must fit in a square of 350*350*350(l*b*h). Dimensions in mm.
    3. The bot must be provided with a proper writing/drawing mechanism.
    4. Human intervention will NOT be allowed at any point during the game.
    POINT SYSTEM : 1. Let the time taken in completing the task be ‘t’ seconds.
    2. Then total marks scored by the team at the end of the game will be equal to (1000-t).
    3. If any letter of the problem statement is not readable, then 100 points will be deducted from the final score of the team.
    VENUE:
    11th March,2016(Day 1)
    Shakuntlam
    Timings:10.00 am-1.00 pm

  • GRAND PRIXX

    Speed !! Speed !! Speed !! In this world of everlasting competition , speed is the word . Blast through the way at an awe inspiring pace and indulge your robust bots in an exciting race . " One , who falls behind, is left behind . " So pack your bags, pump your bots and gear them up to GO !!!
    PROBLEM STATEMENT:
    Use your creative mind to create a remote controlled wired or wireless machine that can cross an artificial path with hurdles and obstacles.
    Team Size:
    1. The maximum number of team members in a team is limited to 4. They may belong to different institutions.
    2. Students must carry a valid college ID card.3. Participants shall not be allowed to be a part of more than one team.

    RULES:
    1. Maximum dimension of robot allowed is 20cm x20cm x 15cm (length x breadth x height).
    2. Robots can be wireless or wired.
    3. Use of IC engine is not allowed. Only electric motors should be used for movement.
    4. There will be a certain number of check points on the track, which will be informed to the participants before the start of the run. If a machine tumbles or goes off the arena at any point of time during the race, then it will be placed back on the last check point through which the machine passed. But the time shall be kept running.

    6. If a robot is unable to move for more than 30s then it will be disqualified.
    5. Organizers will not be responsible for any damage done to the robot in the arena.
    6. If any team is found harming the arena, the team will be disqualified on the spot.
    7. Teams should bring all the requisite spare-parts themselves. No spare-parts will be provided.
    8. In case of any discrepancies, the decision of the event coordinators will be final.
    9. The coordinators reserve the rights to change any of the above rules as they deem fit.

    Judging Criterion :
    1. The winner will be decided on the basis of minimum time taken by the robot to complete the track.
    2. If the robot crosses the boundary, then it will be placed back on the last check point through which the machine passed.
    3. The top 3 teams will be awarded certificates and prizes.
    4. Time measured by any contestant by any other means is not acceptable for scoring. In general, the decision of the organizers will be final and binding in all circumstances.

  • MAZE MANIA

    A maze and a robot! These are the attractions of this event. Come and construct a robot which can traverse a path on its own. Can you do this?
    Maze Mania is an event in which an autonomous robot solves a maze. A maze solver robot is a one which trails in a maze. Sovereign robots are brought into play in this event. In this event robot follows the specified track on the arena. The robot which solves the maze in minimum time is declared the front runner. Each time the robot stops or deviates from the specified path its points are deducted. And finally after evaluation the total score is used to declare the victor.
    GENERAL RULES:
    • Each team can have a maximum of 4 participants.
    • A team may consist of students from different colleges.
    • No damage should be made by a bot to the arena or to other team’s bots during the match in any manner.
    • Bots should not be disassembled until the results are declared.
    • The organizers reserve the right to change the rules as they deem fit.
    • When a team is called for match, they must report within five minutes.
    • Judges' decision will be final.
    • If during line traversal, the bot deviates from the specified path, human intervention will be allowed and points will be deducted for the same.
    • The arena shown is just for reference.
    • The Mat for line following will not be disclosed before the event.
    • The judges can ask for an explanation of any mechanism on the bot and there would be an immediate disqualification of defaulters of any kind.
    Mission Objective:
    The bot must be fully autonomous and has to perform the specified tasks:
    • Initial position: You shall place your bot at point A in any direction, in order to perform the line following task.
    • Robot has to reach point B by solving a maze by the principle of line following.
    • On reaching the point B, the robot has to mark the end of the game by glowing a LED for more than 3 seconds.

    Bot Specifications:
    • During the whole event the bot must fit within a square of 250X250X250 (lXbXh). Dimensions in mm.
    • The Robot must be stable and able to move on its own. A bot not fulfilling these criteria will be disqualified.
    • The bots should be able to follow the line according to event specifications.
    • Each team has to bring its own power supply for its robots.
    • Teams are advised to use an on-board power supply. In case they are using external power supply they will be responsible for any problem created by entanglement of wires.
    • Bot's code will be checked for hard coding before trail is allowed.

    Points System:
    • 10 points will be awarded for crossing check post 1, 20 for check post 2 and 30 for check post 3 (these locations will be shown on the final competition day).
    • 50 points will be awarded for glowing the LED at the end.
    • -5 will be awarded for any sort of human intervention.

    Restarts and Reset:
    • A maximum of 3 restarts will be given.
    • A restart can be taken when human intervention is required.
    • On restart the bot has to begin from the initial position but the timer will continue.
    • A total of 3 resets i.e. 3 chances will be given.
    • During reset the timer will begin from 0.
    • Best score out of the 3 will be considered
    VENUE:
    12th March,2016(Day 2)
    Indoor 1
    Timings:1.00 am-4.30 pm

  • CARRY ON

    A bot to correctly diffuse the boxes and other important materials. Bot will pick them from one side and travel in the desired region and then place the box on the other side. Witness the robots moving through different arenas with different obstacles!
    PROBLEM STATEMENT:
    Create a bot to pick and place objects while tackling various hurdles in the way.
    TEAM SIZE:
    1. A maximum team size of 4 is allowed.
    2. The participants can belong to different institutes.
    3. One person can not be a part of more than one team.
    RULES:
    1. The maximum size of bot should be 30cm*30cm*30cm (arm excluded).
    2. Weight of bot should not exceed 10kg.
    3. A max of 500rpm DC motor is allowed.
    4. Wired or wireless, any kind of bot can be used.
    5. It can be manual or autonomous as the user wants to have.
    6. The maximum length of the robotic arm is 40 cm.
    7. Time penalties will be given for every task that the bot cannot accomplish.
    8. No damage should be made by a bot to the arena or to other team’s bots during the match in any manner.
    9. The arena shown is just for reference.
    10. The judges can ask for an explanation of any mechanism on the bot and there would be an immediate disqualification of defaulters of any kind.
    11. Robots should not be factory made or commercially available.
    12. The wire used to power/feed the bot should remain slack at all times.
    13. Each team has to bring its own power supply for its robots. The voltage difference between any two points on the bot must not exceed 24 volts.
    14. Teams are advised to use an on-board power supply. In case they are using external power supply they will be responsible for any problem created by entanglement of wires.
    TRACK :
    1. The track has three major parts.
    2. First part will have 3 boxes of different colours and 3 cavities of same colors respectively. The user will pick up the boxes and place them in his respective cavity. The block provided will be of 7cm*7cm to 10cm*10cm.
    3. Second part will comprise of an uphill course where the top will have a platform where a block is placed.The bot is required to go up there and hold the block up in the air. And while holding the block it comes down the elevation.Then it places the block in the desired position. The maximum angle of elevation can be of 45 degrees. And the track can be of any material that may include even a slippery one.
    4. Third part and the most interesting one. In this stage the robot has to climb the hill again and then pass through a bridge made up of two parallel beams. On one-third distance of the bridge, there will be a block present in the middle of the beams. The robot has to pick that block, complete it’s journey on the bridge and then it has to place that block at the end point to mark the ending of the game.
    If the robot falls from the bridge then the robot has to climb the hill again and has to cross the bridge again, no previous progress on the bridge will be considered. 5. Black block and beam are on the same height.

    JUDGING CRITERIA :
    1. The bot which finishes the track in the minimum time including the time penalties will be declared as the winner.
    2. The dimensions and weight limit of the bot must be strictly followed. All the bots will be checked before the competition begins. The bots not made according to the rule will be disqualified.
    3. The decision of the co-ordinators will be considered as the final decision.
    4. In case of toppling, the co-ordinators will reposition the bot, the participating members will not do this.

    Points System :
    Points will be calculated in the following manner :
    100 points will be awarded for placing the green block on it’s desired place.
    100 points will be awarded for placing the red block on it’s desired place.
    100 points will be awarded for placing the orange block on it’s desired place.
    200 points will be awarded for placing the blue block on it’s desired place.
    100 points will be awarded for placing the black block on it’s desired place.
    100 points will be awarded as a bonus if all the blocks are placed at their desired places.
    (3*t) will be subtracted from the final score , where t is time in seconds.

  • ROBO RAGE

    It is a robo fighting tournament, where the aim of the participating robot is to demobilize the enemy robot. To hear their applause, you have to go mindless without fear, beat the thrill and be ready for the kill. So are you ready to fight...ready to make the kill ????
    PROBLEM STATEMENT:
    Use your creative mind to create a remote controlled wired or wireless machine that can fight against your opponent.
    Team Size:
    1. The maximum number of members in a team is limited to 4.They may belong to different institutions.
    2. The students must carry valid student ID cards of their colleges which they will be required to produce at the time of registration.
    3. Participants shall not be allowed to be a part of more than one team.

    Gameplay:
    1. Each battle will consist of 3 rounds.
    2. Each round will be of 3 minutes.
    3. Bot will be considered eliminated if it either falls off the arena or is immobile or cannot perform linear movement within 40 seconds.
    4. If at the end of 3 rounds, both the bots are active and inside the arena, result will be declared on the basis of a tie breaker.
    5. Tie-breaker will consist of a ring of reduced diameter and it will be a single round of 1.5 seconds.
    RULES:
    1. The dimension of the arena is a circle of diameter=200cm or 2m and outer boundary area of 220cm or 2.2m.
    2. The dimension of the robot used for the robo rage is 30x30 (length x breadth) (10% tolerance allowed). All dimensions are in cm.
    3. The maximum possible weight for the robot is 10 kg.
    4. Robot should be wired or wireless. In case of wireless robot the battery should be on board.
    5. The robot should not have any attacking weapons. The robot should push the opponent robot outside the arena.
    6. Use of wedges,gripper is allowed to push the opponent robot.
    7. No lifting or flippering and moving of weapon mechanism is allowed.
    8. Jumping, flying & hopping is not allowed.
    9. A robot is declared victorious if its opponent is immobilized.
    10. A robot will be declared immobile if it cannot display linear motion of at least 1 inch in a time period of 40 seconds.
    11. If a robot is thrown out of the arena, the match will stop immediately & the robot still inside the arena will be declared as the winner.
    12. If the robots become entagled with each other, the robots will be taken back to the initial position and the match will begin again.
    13. Certificates of excellence will be awarded to the top three teams.
    14. The organisers reserve the right to change the rules as them deem fit.
    15. Co-ordinators' decision will be final and binding to all.
    VENUE:
    13th March,2016(Day 3)
    Shakuntlam
    Timings:1.00 pm-4.30 pm
